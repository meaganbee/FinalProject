Connecting Queens Immigrant Communities to Social Support Organizations

New York City is home to large and ethnically diverse immigrant communities, with 3.2 million people identified as foreign-born according to the latest report from the NYC Mayor's Office of Immigrant Affairs.

The borough of Queens is home to the largest immigrant population in New York City - approximately 1.1 million immigrants call it home. The following Queens neighborhoods have the biggest immigrant populations:

Jackson Heights
Flushing
Jamaica
Elmhurst
Astoria

With one of the most diverse populations in the world, immigrants from all over the globe live in Queens. A few of the countries represented include (but certainly aren't limited to):

  • China
  • India
  • Ecuador
  • Cameroon
  • Jamaica
  • Dominican Republic
